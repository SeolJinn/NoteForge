# Home Coffee Setup

The little corner of the kitchen I've slowly dialed in, paired with my [[Coffee Brew Methods]] notes.

## The gear I settled on

I resisted buying more than I needed, but a few things were worth it.

- A burr grinder, this mattered more than any other upgrade
- A simple gooseneck kettle for control over the pour
- A basic scale, because eyeballing the dose never worked
- One brewer I actually like, not a cupboard full of gadgets

The grinder is the thing I'd tell anyone to spend on first.

## My morning flow

I like the ritual as much as the coffee, honestly.

- Heat the water while I grind fresh, never pre-ground
- Weigh the beans, same dose every day so I have a baseline
- Brew, sit, and not look at a screen for those few minutes

## Keeping it clean

The dirty secret of home coffee is that stale gear ruins good beans.

- Rinse everything right after brewing, it takes seconds then
- Wipe down the grinder area, grounds get everywhere
- A deeper clean of the kettle and brewer every week or so

## Beans and storage

- I buy small bags so they're always reasonably fresh
- Stored in an airtight container away from light and heat
- I note which beans I liked so I can find them again

## What I'm still tweaking

- Dialing in the grind size for each new bag
- Water temperature, small changes are surprisingly noticeable
- The ratio, I keep nudging it to match how I like it that week
