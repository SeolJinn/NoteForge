# Houseplant Care

The general rules I've picked up keeping things alive, with specifics over in [[Plants I'm Growing]].

## The mistake I kept making

Almost every plant I killed, I killed by loving it too much.

- Overwatering, every single time, not underwatering
- I now wait until the top inch of soil is actually dry
- Soggy roots rot, and a rotted plant rarely comes back

When in doubt, I leave it alone for a few more days.

## Light is everything

I learned to read my windows before placing a plant.

- Bright but indirect suits most of what I keep
- A leaf that's bleaching is getting too much direct sun
- Long, stretchy, reaching growth means it wants more light
- I rotate pots so they grow evenly instead of leaning

## My watering rhythm

I stopped watering on a schedule and started watering on need.

- Finger in the soil, dry knuckle-deep means water
- I water thoroughly until it drains, then not again until dry
- Less in the cold months when growth slows right down

## The boring maintenance

- Wipe dust off the leaves so they can actually breathe
- Snip dead or yellowing leaves to keep energy where it counts
- Repot when roots start creeping out the drainage holes
- A bit of feeding during the growing season, sparingly

Most plant problems I've had came from doing too much, not too little.
