# Fixing a Leaky Faucet

What I did when the bathroom tap started its slow drip and drove me up the wall.

## Before touching anything

The first time I skipped this step and made a mess. Never again.

- Turn off the water at the shutoff valves under the sink
- Open the tap to drain the line and confirm the water's really off
- Plug the drain so no small parts vanish down it
- Lay a towel down and a bowl for the parts as I remove them

## Taking it apart

I go slow and line the parts up in the order they came off.

- Pop off the decorative cap and undo the handle screw
- Lift the handle, then work down to the cartridge or washer
- Take a photo at each stage so reassembly isn't a puzzle
- The worn rubber washer or O-ring is usually the culprit

## The actual fix

Most of my leaks came down to one tired little part.

- Bring the old washer or cartridge to the hardware store to match it
- Replace like for like, a slightly wrong size just leaks again
- A dab of plumber's grease on seals helps them seat and last

## Putting it back

- Reassemble in reverse, snug but not gorilla-tight
- Turn the water back on slowly and watch for drips
- Run it a minute and check underneath the sink for surprises

If it still leaks after all this, that's my cue to call a real plumber.
