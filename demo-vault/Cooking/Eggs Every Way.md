# Eggs Every Way

The cheapest test of whether you can actually cook. Heat control is everything here.

## Scrambled
Low heat, constant stirring, pull them off while still glossy and slightly wet. They finish on the plate. High heat gives rubbery curds.

## Soft Boiled
- Straight from the fridge into already boiling water.
- 6.5 minutes for a set white and a runny yolk.
- Ice bath the second they come out so they peel clean.

## Fried
- Sunny side up: low heat, lid on, no flip, the steam sets the top.
- Crispy: hot oil, spoon the fat over the edges until they frill and brown.

## Omelette
Beaten eggs into a hot buttered pan, stir for a few seconds, then leave to set and fold. French style stays pale, no color.

## Lessons Learned
- Fresh eggs poach far better. Older ones spread into wispy clouds.
- Season scrambled eggs at the end. Salt early can make them watery.
- A nonstick pan and real butter solve most egg problems.
