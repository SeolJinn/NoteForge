# Pasta from Scratch

Egg pasta dough, no machine necessary if you commit to the rolling pin. Companion to [[Knife Skills]].

## The Dough

100g of "00" flour per egg. One egg makes a generous single portion. Pile the flour, make a well, crack the egg into it, beat with a fork while gradually pulling flour in from the walls. When it's a shaggy mass, abandon the fork and knead with the heel of your hand for 8 minutes. Wrap, rest 30 minutes at room temp. The rest is non-negotiable — the gluten needs to relax or the dough will fight you when you roll it.

## Rolling

Dust the surface, flatten with the rolling pin, then roll outward from the center, rotating the disc 30 degrees between passes. Keep going until you can read newsprint through it. This takes longer than you think the first few times.

## Shapes

- **Tagliatelle.** Roll the sheet into a loose cylinder, slice across, unfurl. About 8mm wide.
- **Pappardelle.** Same idea, 2-3cm wide.
- **Garganelli.** Squares rolled around a dowel over a ridged board. Most labor-intensive, holds sauce best.

## Cooking

Salt the water aggressively — it should taste like the sea. Fresh pasta cooks in 90 seconds to 2 minutes. Reserve a mug of pasta water before draining. Finish the pasta in the pan with the sauce, splashing in pasta water until it emulsifies.

## When Not to Bother

Long-cooked tomato sauces, ragus, anything where the sauce dominates — store-bought dried pasta is honestly fine. Save the fresh stuff for butter-and-cheese, simple herb sauces, and stuffed shapes.
