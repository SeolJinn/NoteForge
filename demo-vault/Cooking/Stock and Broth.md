# Stock and Broth

Two different things despite the menu confusion. See [[Sourdough Bread]] for the patience analogue.

## Definitions That Actually Hold Up

- **Stock** is made primarily from bones, simmered for hours. Gelatinous when cold. Used as a building block.
- **Broth** is made from meat (and vegetables), shorter cook, lighter body, drinkable on its own.

## Brown Chicken Stock

Roast 2kg of chicken backs and wings at 220C until deep mahogany — roughly 45 minutes. Deglaze the pan with a splash of water, scrape, dump everything into a stockpot. Add cold water to cover by 5cm, one onion (skin on, halved), two carrots, two ribs of celery, a handful of parsley stems, a bay leaf, ten peppercorns. Bring to a bare simmer, never boil. Skim the scum that rises in the first 30 minutes. Simmer 4-6 hours. Strain twice — once through a colander, once through a sieve lined with cheesecloth. Cool fast over an ice bath, then refrigerate.

## Common Errors

- Boiling instead of simmering. Boiling emulsifies the fat into the liquid; you want clear stock.
- Not skimming. The cloudy gray foam on top is denatured proteins; skim it off and the finished stock is glassy.
- Salting during the cook. Salt at the dish, not the stock — the stock will reduce in unpredictable ways.

## Storage

Refrigerate up to 5 days, freeze up to 3 months in 1-cup portions. The fat cap that solidifies on top is a feature: skim it off and you have schmaltz.
