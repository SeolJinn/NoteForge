# Coffee Brew Methods

Notes from too many mornings spent fiddling.

## V60 (Pour Over)

- 15g coffee, medium-fine grind.
- 250g water at 94C.
- 30s bloom with 50g, then pour in two stages: to 150g over the next 30s, to 250g over the next 60s.
- Total brew time about 3 minutes.

The cleanest, most articulate cup of the methods I have at home. Best for light roasts where the fruit and acidity are the point.

## AeroPress

- 17g coffee, medium grind.
- 250g water at 90C, inverted.
- Pour, stir, steep 90 seconds, flip, press over 30 seconds.

Forgiving and fast. I take it traveling. Makes a cup closer to espresso in body without actually being one.

## French Press

- 30g coffee, coarse grind.
- 500g water at 95C.
- Stir at 1 minute, plunge at 4 minutes, decant immediately.

The boldest, fullest body. Best for darker roasts. Decant — leaving the brewed coffee on the grounds will turn it bitter within minutes.

## Espresso (When I Have a Real Machine)

- 18g in, 36g out, 28-32 seconds.
- Adjust grind first, dose second, time third.

A bad espresso machine produces worse coffee than any of the above. Don't buy one until you're ready for the rabbit hole.

## What I Stopped Doing

- Weighing every variable. Once you've calibrated, eyeballing is fine for daily use.
- Buying single-origin beans I read about online. The roaster matters more than the origin.
- Pre-ground coffee. The drop in quality is real and immediate.
