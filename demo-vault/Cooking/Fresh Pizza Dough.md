# Fresh Pizza Dough

A cold-fermented dough that beats anything I can order. The slow rise is the whole secret, same logic as [[Sourdough Bread]].

## Formula (four pizzas)
- 600g 00 flour
- 390g water (65% hydration)
- 12g salt
- 2g instant yeast

## The Rise
This is where flavor comes from. Mix, knead briefly, then bulk ferment 1 hour at room temp before splitting into balls.

## Cold Fermentation
- Ball the dough, oil lightly, into the fridge.
- 24 to 72 hours. The yeast works slowly and develops a deep, faintly sour taste.
- Pull it out 2 hours before baking so it comes to room temperature and relaxes.

## Bake
Hottest the oven goes, a stone or steel preheated 45 minutes. Stretch by hand, never roll, to keep the gas in the rim.

## Lessons Learned
- Wet dough is good dough. Resist adding flour while kneading.
- A slack, bubbly ball after the fridge means the ferment worked.
- Underproofed dough fights back and snaps. Let it rest and try again.
