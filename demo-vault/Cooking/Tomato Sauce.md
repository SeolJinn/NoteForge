# Tomato Sauce

The one sauce worth knowing cold. I make a big batch and lean on it all week, often over [[Pasta from Scratch]].

## Two Versions
- Quick (20 min): garlic in oil, canned tomatoes, salt, basil. Bright and fresh.
- Slow (90 min): soffritto base, low simmer, deep and sweet.

## Canned Beats Fresh
For most of the year, good canned San Marzano tomatoes beat sad supermarket fresh ones. I crush whole peeled by hand rather than buy pre-chopped.

## Building Flavor
- Bloom the garlic in cold oil over low heat until it just turns gold. Burnt garlic ruins the pot.
- A pinch of sugar only if the tomatoes are sharp, never by default.
- Tear basil in at the end so it stays fragrant.

## Lessons Learned
- Salt early and taste late. The sauce concentrates as it reduces.
- Finish with a knob of butter or a glug of raw oil for body.
- It freezes beautifully in flat bags. I always make double.
