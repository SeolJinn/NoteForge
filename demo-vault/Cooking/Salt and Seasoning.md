# Salt and Seasoning

The single biggest lever on flavor, and the one most home cooks underuse. See [[Nutrition Basics]] before assuming all salt is the enemy.

## Salt by Weight, Not Volume
Table salt and flaky salt have wildly different volumes per gram. I keep a box of kosher salt for cooking and a small jar of flaky salt for finishing.

## When to Salt
- Meat: well ahead, ideally the night before. Surface dries and seasons through.
- Pasta water: until it tastes like the sea, then a little more.
- Vegetables: at the start of cooking so it has time to draw moisture and concentrate.

## Beyond Salt
- Acid for brightness. Lemon, vinegar, a fermented thing.
- Fat carries flavor. A flat dish is often just underfatted.
- Heat layers slowly. Add chili early for warmth, late for bite.

## Lessons Learned
- Taste constantly and adjust in small steps. You can add but you cannot remove.
- If a dish tastes dull but is salty enough, it almost always needs acid.
- Season in layers at every stage, not one big dump at the end.
