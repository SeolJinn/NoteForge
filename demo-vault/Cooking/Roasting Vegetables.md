# Roasting Vegetables

My default way to make almost any vegetable taste good. Cross-reference [[Salt and Seasoning]] when things come out flat.

## The Two Rules
- Heat. 220°C minimum, often 230°C. Low oven steams, high oven caramelizes.
- Space. Crowd the pan and everything sweats. One layer, gaps between pieces, two trays if needed.

## Timing by Type
- Dense roots (carrot, potato, beet): 35-45 min, cut to 2cm chunks.
- Cruciferous (broccoli, cauliflower, sprouts): 20-25 min, flat sides down.
- Quick stuff (zucchini, peppers, cherry tomatoes): 15-20 min.

## Fat
Toss in enough oil to coat with a slight sheen, roughly a tablespoon per tray. Too little and they go leathery, too much and they fry greasy.

## Lessons Learned
- Salt before roasting, not after. It needs time to penetrate.
- Don't flip too early. Let a crust form first or you tear it off.
- Acid at the end wakes everything up. A squeeze of lemon or a splash of vinegar off the hot tray.
