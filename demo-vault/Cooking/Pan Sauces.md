# Pan Sauces

How to turn the brown stuff stuck to a pan into dinner. Pairs naturally with [[Braising Tough Cuts]] when I want something quicker.

## The Sequence
1. Sear meat, remove, rest it.
2. Pour off most of the fat, leave the fond.
3. Sweat a chopped shallot for a minute.
4. Deglaze with liquid, scrape every brown bit loose.
5. Reduce, then mount with cold butter off the heat.

## Liquids I Reach For
- Dry white wine for chicken and pork.
- Red wine or stock for beef.
- A splash of vinegar plus water when I have nothing else.

## Mounting Butter
Off the heat, swirl in cold cubes one at a time. The sauce goes glossy and thickens slightly. Boil it after this and it breaks.

## Lessons Learned
- The fond is flavor. Burnt black fond is bitter, so watch the sear.
- Reduce by about half before tasting for salt. Concentrating changes everything.
- A 5cm sauce takes 5 minutes. No excuse to serve naked meat.
