# Braising Tough Cuts

Low and slow turns the cheapest, chewiest cuts into the best meals I make. Borrow the fond trick from [[Pan Sauces]] for the braising liquid.

## Why It Works
Tough cuts are full of collagen. Hold them at a gentle simmer long enough and it melts into gelatin, which is where all that silky richness comes from.

## My Standard Process
1. Pat the meat bone dry, salt it, sear hard on every side.
2. Build a base of onion, carrot, celery in the same pot.
3. Deglaze with wine, add stock to come halfway up the meat.
4. Lid on, into a 150°C oven for 2.5 to 3 hours.

## Cuts I Trust
- Beef short rib and chuck.
- Pork shoulder.
- Lamb shank.

## Lessons Learned
- Do not boil. A hard boil makes the meat stringy and tight. Bare bubbles only.
- It is better the next day. The fat sets and you can lift it off cold.
- Done is when a fork twists out with no resistance, not a clock time.
