# Sourdough Bread

Working notes on a high-hydration country loaf. See also [[Stock and Broth]] for fermentation parallels.

## Starter

50g flour, 50g water, daily feed. Healthy when it doubles in 4-6 hours and smells faintly of yogurt. If it smells like nail polish, it's hungry — feed twice today.

## Formula (one loaf)

- 400g bread flour
- 100g whole wheat
- 380g water (76% hydration)
- 100g active starter
- 10g salt

## Timeline

- 9:00 — autolyse flour and water for 45 min
- 9:45 — add starter, salt, mix
- 10:00–13:00 — bulk ferment, four sets of stretch-and-folds at 30-min intervals
- 13:30 — preshape, rest 20 min
- 13:50 — final shape, into banneton
- Cold retard overnight in the fridge
- Next morning — preheat dutch oven to 250C, bake 20 min covered, 20 min uncovered

## Lessons Learned

- Underproofed loaves are gummy and dense. Trust the float test on the dough, not the clock.
- Score deeper than you think. A timid score springs up into a flat-topped boule.
- Cool fully on a rack before slicing. Hot bread sets the crumb wrong.
