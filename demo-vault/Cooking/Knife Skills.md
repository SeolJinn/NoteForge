# Knife Skills

The least glamorous and highest-leverage thing in cooking. Pairs with [[Pasta from Scratch]].

## The Pinch Grip

Thumb and index finger pinch the blade just past the bolster. The other three fingers wrap the handle. This sounds fussy and isn't — it puts your hand directly behind the cutting edge and dramatically increases control.

## The Claw

Curl your fingertips back, knuckles forward. The blade rides against the flat of your knuckles. You physically cannot cut yourself if you maintain the claw — the knife has nowhere to go.

## Cuts Worth Memorizing

- **Brunoise** — 1-2mm dice. Tedious but disciplined.
- **Julienne** — matchsticks, ~3mm × 3mm × 4cm.
- **Chiffonade** — stack leaves, roll, slice. For basil, mint.
- **Rough chop** — when uniformity doesn't matter and speed does.

## Sharpening

Whetstones, not pull-throughs. A 1000/4000 combo stone covers most needs. Three minutes a week beats half an hour every six months. A truly sharp knife is safer than a dull one — less force, less slip.

## The Most Common Mistake

Trying to cut faster than your free hand can move. Speed comes from rhythm, not from rushing. Watch a line cook — the knife hand looks unhurried; the other hand is what's actually fast.
