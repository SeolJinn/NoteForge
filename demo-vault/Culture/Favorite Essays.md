# Favorite Essays

Pieces I reread and force on other people. Overlaps with my [[Quotes Collection]].

## The ones I've reread most

- "The Patience of Maps" by Della Hart - about getting lost on purpose. I think about it on every walk now.
- "On Keeping a Slow Notebook" by Renzo A[last name escapes me] - the case for writing things down badly and often.
- "What the River Forgot" by Mira Solenne - ostensibly about hydrology, actually about memory. Sneaky.

## Essays that changed how I work

- "Against the Tidy Desk" - a small permission slip to leave things half-finished. I needed it.
- "The Two-Hour Rule" by Cass Whitlow - argues most good ideas die in the third hour, not the first. Mostly true for me.

## Short ones worth the five minutes

- "A Defense of Boredom" - punchy, a little smug, completely right.
- "Letters I Never Sent" by Ines Kovac - barely an essay, more a mood. Stays with me anyway.

## Essays I argue with

- "The Productive Self" - I disagree with almost every line and I've read it four times. That has to mean something.
- "Why We Collect" - good question, weak answer, but it got me thinking about my own hoarding of these lists.

## Why I keep this list

- Half the value of an essay is remembering you read it. Otherwise it just evaporates.
- I'd rather have ten I can quote than a hundred I half-skimmed.
