# Movie Recommendations

Things I keep telling people to watch. No festival darlings nobody has heard of — actual recommendations.

## If You Liked Slow, Beautiful, Sad

- *In the Mood for Love* (2000) — Wong Kar-wai. The standard-bearer.
- *Past Lives* (2023) — Celine Song. The closest a recent film gets to that energy.
- *The Banshees of Inisherin* (2022) — McDonagh. Mean and tender at once.

## If You Want to Laugh

- *What We Do in the Shadows* (2014). Original film, not the (also good) show.
- *Hot Fuzz* (2007). Best of the Cornetto trilogy. Yes, better than Shaun.
- *The Grand Budapest Hotel* (2014). Wes Anderson's most rewatchable.

## If You Want to Be Tense

- *Sicario* (2015) — Villeneuve. The border crossing scene alone.
- *Uncut Gems* (2019) — Safdies. Stress disguised as a movie.
- *Whiplash* (2014) — Chazelle. Closer to a thriller than a music film.

## Underseen

- *Locke* (2013). Tom Hardy. One car, ninety minutes, no flashbacks.
- *Margin Call* (2011). The financial crisis in real time. No actor over- or under-acts.
- *Burning* (2018). Korean. Slow. Stays with you for weeks.

## My One Hill

The Lord of the Rings trilogy is better than every single Marvel film, and not by a small margin.
