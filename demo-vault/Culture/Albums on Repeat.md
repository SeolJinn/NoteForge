# Albums on Repeat

What I keep coming back to. Loosely tied to my [[Concerts I've Seen]] list.

## On heavy rotation right now

- *Slow Tides* by Marah Vell - autumnal folk that I put on every grey morning. The third track ruins me every time.
- *Neon Cartography* by The Glass Atlas - synthy and a bit cold, perfect for late-night work sessions.
- *Held Light* by Osei Bloom - one of those records where the production is the whole point. Headphones only.

## Comfort albums I never skip

- *Roomful of Teeth* sessions - the kind of thing I put on when I can't decide what I want.
- *Brushfire Sessions* by Cal Renner - recorded live in a barn, you can hear the room. That's the appeal.
- *Quiet Machinery* by Iola - minimal, repetitive, gets me into a focus state faster than coffee.

## Older stuff I rediscovered this year

- *Driftwood Mass* (2009) - I ignored this for a decade and now I can't believe I waited.
- *Paper Lanterns* by Senna Voss - aged better than almost anything from that era.

## Albums I respect but rarely finish

- *The Long Inventory* - brilliant for twenty minutes, exhausting by the end. A whole album of ideas with no breathing room.
- *Concrete Hymns* - technically stunning, emotionally I bounce right off it.

## Rules I've noticed about my own taste

- A great opener buys a lot of goodwill. A weak closer is almost forgivable.
- I trust records that sound like a room more than ones that sound like a screen.
- If I'm humming a melody two days later, it's already won.
