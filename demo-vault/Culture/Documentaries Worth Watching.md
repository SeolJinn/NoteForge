# Documentaries Worth Watching

Ones I'd actually recommend, not just nod about. Cross-pollinates with [[Movie Recommendations]].

## Watched recently and loved

- *The Salt Road* - about a centuries-old trade route and the people still walking it. Gorgeous and quiet.
- *Glass and Fire* - a glassblowing doc that's secretly about obsession. The close-up shots are hypnotic.
- *Three Winters* - follows one family across three brutal seasons. No narration, just trust in the footage.

## The "watch with someone" picks

- *Stadium Ghosts* - abandoned arenas and the cities they left behind. Great for a melancholy mood.
- *Hands That Build* - profiles of people who make things by hand. Warm, no cynicism.

## Bigger swings

- *Deep Field* - astronomy doc that genuinely made me feel small in a good way.
- *The Algorithm Ate My Town* - a bit alarmist, but the questions stuck with me longer than I expected.

## Ones I have reservations about

- *True Crime, Again* - well made, but I'm tired of the genre dressing up tragedy as entertainment.
- *The Visionary* - a flattering portrait that forgets to ask hard questions. Pretty, hollow.

## What makes a doc work for me

- Trust the audience. Stop telling me how to feel.
- A real subject beats a flashy one. I'll watch anything if the access is intimate enough.
- Length is a choice. Most of these would be better twenty minutes shorter.
