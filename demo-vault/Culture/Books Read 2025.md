# Books Read 2025

The wrap-up. Continues into [[Reading List 2026]].

## Standouts

- ***A Gentleman in Moscow*** — Amor Towles. A novel that earns its sentimentality. The single best book of the year.
- ***Stoner*** — John Williams. Quiet, devastating. Read it in two sittings.
- ***The Goldfinch*** — Donna Tartt. Long, occasionally indulgent, ultimately worth it.

## Solid

- *Project Hail Mary* — Andy Weir. Unfussy, fun, exactly what it advertises.
- *Klara and the Sun* — Ishiguro. Late Ishiguro is somehow both restrained and crushing.
- *The Body Keeps the Score* — van der Kolk. Heavier than the airport-paperback aesthetic suggests.
- *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* — Kleppmann. Re-read. Still the best technical book on backend engineering.

## Disappointments

- *Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, and Tomorrow* — Zevin. Loved by everyone I trust. Bounced off it.
- *The Bee Sting* — Murray. The structure is virtuosic and the characters are fine but I never cared.

## Did Not Finish

- *The Overstory* — Powers. The trees were the most interesting characters and they don't get enough page time.
- *Sapiens* — Harari. The further in I got, the less I trusted any specific claim.

## Stats

- 27 books finished, 4 DNF'd.
- 60% fiction, 30% non-fiction, 10% poetry.
- Most read on the e-reader, four on paper, two on audio.
- Best sentence: from *Stoner*, but no quoting it without context.
