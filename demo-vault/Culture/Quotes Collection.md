# Quotes Collection

Lines I've stolen and want to keep. Many came from my [[Favorite Essays]] and [[Books Read 2025]].

## On work and making things

- "Finish badly, then finish better." - I tell myself this whenever a draft stalls.
- "The cure for a blank page is a worse page." - from an essay I can't relocate. Annoyingly true.
- "You don't find time, you trade for it." - blunt, and it reset how I plan my weeks.

## On attention

- "What you look at, you become." - heard it secondhand, never confirmed the source, still believe it.
- "Boredom is the doorway, not the room." - I keep this one near my desk.

## On people

- "Be easy to love and hard to fool." - someone's grandmother, allegedly. Good advice regardless.
- "Most arguments are two people guarding different wounds." - this reframed a lot of fights for me.

## On time passing

- "We don't remember days, we remember moments." - cliche now, but it earned its place.
- "Nostalgia is just a memory with the boring parts cut out." - cynical and correct.

## Unattributed and probably misremembered

- "Travel light, but pack the right book."
- "A list is a promise to your future self."
- "The best notebook is the one you actually open."

## Why I keep these

- Half of them I can't source and I've stopped caring. If a line is useful, it's mine now.
