# Language Resources

The shortlist that survived after I deleted everything else.

## For Conversation

- **iTalki** for tutors. Community tutors are cheap and plenty good for practice; reserve the certified ones for when you want structure.
- **Tandem** for free language exchange when the budget is tight. Slower, but real people.

A live human you have to respond to in real time is worth more than any app. Everything below is in service of that.

## For Listening

- **Dreaming Spanish** style comprehensible input — graded video where you understand most of it.
- Native podcasts with transcripts. I keep three on rotation and shadow lines from them.
- TV with target-language subtitles. News is clear and slow; reality TV is fast and slangy.

## For Vocab and Grammar

- **Anki** for everything I want to remember long-term.
- **Tatoeba** for example sentences when a word feels abstract.
- A single grammar reference I consult, never read cover to cover.

## Dictionaries

- **WordReference** for the forum threads, which catch nuance no dictionary does.
- A monolingual dictionary once I'm intermediate. Defining a word in the target language is its own exercise.

## What I Uninstalled

- The gamified apps. Fun, addictive, and oddly bad at producing speakers.
- Grammar apps that drill conjugation tables in a vacuum.
- Anything that promised fluency in a number of days.
