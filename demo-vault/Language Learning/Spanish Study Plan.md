# Spanish Study Plan

The plan I actually follow, not the ambitious one I wrote in January. Built around [[Spaced Repetition for Vocab]].

## The Goal

Comfortable conversation by the end of the year. Not fluency, not C1, just being able to order, argue about football, and follow a slow podcast without panicking. Reading and writing come along for free; speaking is the bottleneck, so speaking gets the most reps.

## Daily Minimum

- 15 minutes of flashcards, first thing, before email.
- 10 minutes of audio while making coffee. Comprehensible input, slightly above my level.
- One sentence written and corrected. Just one. It compounds.

The minimum is sacred. Big study days are a bonus, never the plan.

## Weekly

- Two conversation sessions on iTalki, 30 minutes each. This is the part that actually moves the needle.
- One episode of a TV show with Spanish subtitles, not English.
- Mine new vocab from real material, not pre-made lists.

## What's Working

- Talking to a tutor twice a week did more in two months than a year of apps did before.
- Subtitles in the target language, never the native one. English subtitles teach you to read English fast.
- Studying grammar only when a mistake keeps biting me, not front-loaded.

## What I Dropped

- Streak-chasing apps. The owl guilt-tripped me into busywork.
- Grammar drills in isolation. I learn the rule faster after I've felt the gap.
- Trying to "finish" a textbook. There is no finish line.
