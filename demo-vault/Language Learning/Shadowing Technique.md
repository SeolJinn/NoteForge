# Shadowing Technique

The single drill that fixed my accent and my listening at the same time.

## What It Actually Is

You play native audio and speak along with it, a beat behind, copying everything — rhythm, melody, where the speaker rushes and where they pause. Not translating, not understanding every word. Mimicking. It feels ridiculous and works anyway.

## How I Do It

- Pick a short clip, 30 to 60 seconds, from something I've already understood.
- Listen through once cold.
- Shadow it five or six times, eyes off the text after the first pass.
- Record myself once at the end and wince at the playback.

## Picking Material

- Audio with a transcript so I can check the bits I mumble.
- A single speaker I like the sound of. I end up borrowing their voice.
- Slightly too fast on purpose. Easy audio doesn't stretch anything.

Podcasts made for learners are good to start; real native speech is where it has to end up.

## Why It Works For Me

Reading the language never trained my mouth. Shadowing forces the muscles to do what the ears hear, and somewhere in the repetition the prosody stops being foreign. My listening jumped as a side effect — you can't shadow what you can't parse.

## Mistakes I Made

- Shadowing material I didn't understand first. Garbage in, confident garbage out.
- Staring at the transcript the whole time. The eyes cheat; the ears need to lead.
- Doing it silently in my head. The whole point is the mouth.
