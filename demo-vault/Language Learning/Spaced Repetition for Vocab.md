# Spaced Repetition for Vocab

How I run my flashcards without burning out. Feeds directly into the [[Spanish Study Plan]].

## The Tool

Anki, plain and stubborn. The phone app for reviews on the go, the desktop for making cards. I tried fancier apps with prettier UIs and always crawled back. The algorithm is the product; everything else is decoration.

## Card Rules

- One fact per card. If a card has two things to recall, it's two cards.
- Always with audio. A word I can't hear isn't a word I know.
- Context sentence on the back, not just the translation. Words live in sentences.
- A picture instead of an English word when I can. Skip the translation layer.

## Mining, Not Memorizing Lists

I make cards from things I actually read or heard — a podcast line, a subtitle, a tutor's correction. Pre-made decks felt like studying someone else's vocabulary. Mined cards stick because they already have a memory attached.

## Keeping Reviews Sane

- Hard cap of 20 new cards a day. More than that and tomorrow's pile buries me.
- If a card fails four times, I "leech" it — suspend it and rebuild it differently.
- Reviews happen in the morning, never as a backlog at midnight.

## What I Got Wrong Early

- Adding 50 new cards a day in week one. The avalanche arrived on schedule.
- Cards with the English word on the front. Recognition is easy and useless; production is the point.
- Editing the algorithm's intervals because I thought I knew better. I did not.
