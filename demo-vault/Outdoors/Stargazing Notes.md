# Stargazing Notes

Notes from nights out with a cheap pair of binoculars and a lot of patience. No telescope yet.

## Getting Started Right

- Binoculars beat a bad telescope. My 10x50s show way more than I expected.
- Let my eyes dark-adapt for a full 20 minutes. No phone screen in that window.
- A red flashlight preserves night vision. White light ruins it instantly.
- Drove 40 minutes out of town to escape the light dome. Night and day difference.

## What I Can Find

- The Moon's terminator. Craters pop along that shadow line.
- Jupiter and its four big moons, lined up like a tiny solar system.
- The Orion Nebula, a faint smudge below the belt. My favorite target.
- The Pleiades. A whole cluster that fills the binocular view.

## Star-Hopping

- I use the Big Dipper to find Polaris, then work outward from there.
- Arc to Arcturus, then speed on to Spica. That old saying actually works.
- A planisphere for the date beats fumbling with an app outside.

## Gear Wishlist

- A reclining chair so my neck stops aching.
- A proper star atlas, not just the phone.
- Eventually an 8-inch Dobsonian. Saving up.

## Logging

- I note the date, the seeing conditions, and what I spotted.
- Best nights are cold and crisp right after a front passes through.
