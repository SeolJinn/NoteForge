# Camping Checklist

My running list for weekend car-camping trips, refined after a few cold, hungry nights. Overlaps with my [[Packing List]] for the basics.

## Shelter & Sleep

- 3-season tent, footprint, and an extra stake bag (I always lose two).
- Sleeping bag rated 10 degrees colder than I expect to need.
- Inflatable pad plus a cheap foam pad underneath. The cold comes from below.
- Camp pillow. Stuffing clothes in a sack never works for me.

## Kitchen

- Two-burner propane stove and a spare canister.
- One pot, one pan, a spork, and a sharp knife. That's plenty.
- Cooler with block ice, not cubes. Lasts twice as long.
- Biodegradable soap, a sponge, and a quick-dry towel.

## Light & Power

- Headlamp for everyone, plus a lantern for the table.
- Power bank, fully charged. Phone is the map and camera.
- Spare batteries in a labeled bag.

## The Stuff I Always Forget

- A lighter *and* matches in a ziplock.
- Trash bags. Pack it in, pack it out.
- A camp chair. Sitting on a log gets old by hour two.
- Bug spray and a small first aid kit.

## Pre-Trip Routine

- Check the weather the morning of, not three days before.
- Confirm there's a fire ban or not.
- Freeze water bottles to double as cooler ice.
