# Bouldering Basics

Six months into climbing at the local gym and I'm hooked. These are notes to my past self.

## What Clicked Early

- It's legs, not arms. I wasted my first month death-gripping everything.
- Straight arms whenever possible. Hanging off bone saves the forearms.
- Read the route from the ground before touching it.
- Quiet feet. If I'm scraping and slipping I'm not committing to my toes.

## Grades I'm Working

- Cruising V1 and V2 now.
- V3 is the wall I'm hitting, usually on crimpy or overhung problems.
- Sent my first V4 last week. A slab with one terrifying high step.

## Technique To Drill

- Flagging to stop the dreaded "barn door" swing.
- Heel hooks. So powerful once I trust them.
- Drop knees on the overhang to get my hips into the wall.

## Gear

- Aggressive downturned shoes were overkill. Got flatter ones, much happier.
- Chalk bag with a brush loop for cleaning greasy holds.
- That's genuinely it. No rope, no rack. Love the simplicity.

## Staying Healthy

- Warm up properly. A tweaked pulley took three weeks to heal.
- No campus board until I'm a year in. My tendons aren't ready.
- Rest days are non-negotiable. I climb three days a week, max.
