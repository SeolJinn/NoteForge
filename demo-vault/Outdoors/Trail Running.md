# Trail Running

Switched from road to trails last spring and my knees thank me. Form notes live in [[Running Form]].

## Why Trails Won Me Over

- Softer ground, way less impact than pavement pounding.
- I actually look up and notice things instead of zoning out.
- Hills sneak in serious fitness gains without me trying.
- Almost nobody else out there at 7am.

## My Regular Routes

- Cedar Ridge loop: 6km, one brutal climb in the first kilometer.
- River Path: flat and fast, my go-to for an easy recovery day.
- Summit Out-and-Back: 12km, I save this for Saturdays.

## Gear That Matters

- Trail shoes with real lugs. Road shoes slid out on the first wet descent.
- A 1L vest with soft flasks. Hand-held bottles drove me nuts.
- Gaiters for the gravelly sections. No more stopping to dig out stones.

## Technique Notes

- Shorten my stride uphill and just power-hike the steep stuff. No shame.
- On descents I lean forward and let gravity do the work. Scary but faster.
- Eyes about three meters ahead, not at my feet. Fewer trips that way.

## Lessons Learned The Hard Way

- Tell someone the route before a solo long run.
- Carry more water than I think I need on hot days.
- A rolled ankle ended my season once. I do balance drills now.
