# Time Blocking

Putting work on the calendar instead of a to-do list. Where most of my [[Deep Work Sessions]] get protected.

## Why I Do It
- A task list is infinite. A day is not. Blocking forces the math.
- If it isn't on the calendar it doesn't happen. Harsh but true for me.
- Seeing the day full makes me say no to new things automatically.

## How I Block
- Mornings for the hard stuff, always. My brain is gone by 3pm.
- Admin and email in one ugly afternoon block, never sprinkled.
- Leave gaps on purpose. A perfectly packed day shatters at the first surprise.

## What Goes Wrong
- I overestimate how much fits. Everything takes 1.5x what I guessed.
- Treating blocks as sacred until the first real interruption, then abandoning the whole day.
- Blocking "work on project" with no specific first step. Useless. Be concrete or it's decoration.

## The Adjustment
- I now block in half-days, not 30-minute slivers. The fine-grained version was a lie.
- If a block slips, I move it once. If it slips twice, the task is wrong, not the schedule.

## Honest Take
Time blocking works, but not the Instagram color-coded version. Mine is three or four ugly blocks and a lot of whitespace. That's the part that actually holds up.
