# Saying No

The skill I'm worst at and the one that buys back the most time. Notes to keep myself honest.

## Why It's Hard
- Saying yes feels generous in the moment and costs nothing today.
- The cost shows up three weeks later when I'm drowning and confused why.
- I confuse being liked with being useful. They're not the same.

## Things That Help Me Say It
- "Let me check my calendar" instead of an instant yes. Buys a beat to think.
- Remembering every yes is a no to something already on my plate.
- If it's not a clear yes, it's a no. The maybes are almost always nos in disguise.

## Scripts I Actually Use
- "I can't take this on right now, but thanks for thinking of me."
- "That's not something I can do well, so I'd rather not do it badly."
- No long explanation. The over-explaining is just me asking permission to decline.

## What I'm Still Learning
- Saying no early is kind. Saying yes then flaking later is the real rudeness.
- A delayed no is worse than an immediate one. I sit on them and that helps nobody.

## The Reframe
Every commitment I keep is one I should have weighed before agreeing, not after. No is a complete sentence. I keep relearning that one.
