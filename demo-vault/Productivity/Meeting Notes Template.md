# Meeting Notes Template

The skeleton I copy before every meeting so I'm not scribbling chaos. Fill it in live, clean it up never.

## Header
- **Date:**
- **Attendees:**
- **Purpose (one line):**

## Before The Meeting
- What do I actually need out of this? Write it here first.
- The one question that, if answered, makes the meeting worth it.

## Notes
- Decisions, not discussion. Nobody rereads who-said-what.
- Mark disagreements clearly so I remember they weren't resolved.
- If I'm writing a paragraph, I've stopped listening. Keep it to fragments.

## Action Items
- [ ] Task — owner — due date
- [ ] Task — owner — due date

Every action needs a name attached or it belongs to nobody, which means me, which means it dies.

## After
- Send the action items within the hour or they evaporate.
- Anything that needs real thought gets pulled out into its own note, not buried here.

## Why I Use A Template
Starting from blank means I forget the boring-but-critical parts, like writing down who owns what. The template carries the discipline so I don't have to remember it under pressure.
