# Deep Work Sessions

How I actually get hard things done. Pairs well with [[Time Blocking]] and an empty [[Inbox Zero]].

## What Counts As Deep Work
- Anything where I have to hold the whole problem in my head at once.
- Writing the first draft. Editing is shallower, I can do that tired.
- Debugging something gnarly. Not answering Slack about it.

## My Setup
- Phone in another room. Not face-down on the desk. Another room.
- One tab open, ideally none. Browser is where focus goes to die.
- Headphones on even in silence. It's a signal to other people more than to me.
- A single sentence written down: what does "done" look like for this block.

## Session Length
- 90 minutes is my real ceiling. Anything I schedule past that is fiction.
- Two good blocks a day is a great day. Three is rare and I shouldn't plan for it.
- I stopped chasing the four-hour monk sessions people post about. Never once happened.

## What Breaks It
- Starting before I know the first concrete step. I just stare.
- "Quick" checks. There is no quick check.
- Meetings sliced into the morning. A 10am call kills the whole 9-to-11 block, not just the half hour.

## After the Block
- Write down where I stopped so future-me doesn't reload everything cold.
- Don't immediately reward myself with the phone. That trains the wrong loop.
