# Procrastination Notes

What I've figured out about why I stall. Less a fix, more a field guide to my own avoidance.

## What It Actually Is For Me
- Not laziness. It's usually fear that the work will come out bad.
- Sometimes the task is genuinely unclear and stalling is a signal, not a flaw.
- Mostly it's that the first step is too big to start.

## Tricks That Sometimes Work
- Shrink the task until it's embarrassingly small. "Open the file." That's the whole goal.
- The two-minute version: do a worse, faster version first to break the seal.
- Start in the middle if the beginning is the scary part. Momentum doesn't care about order.

## Tricks That Don't Work
- Waiting to feel motivated. The feeling shows up after starting, never before.
- New apps and systems. That's just procrastination wearing a productivity costume.
- Punishing myself. Guilt has never once made me start anything.

## The Real Pattern
When I'm avoiding something hard, I do small useful things instead so it feels like work. Cleaning my desk. Reorganizing notes. It's productive procrastination and it's the sneakiest kind.

## What I Tell Myself
The task is never as bad as the dread of the task. Five minutes in, the dread is gone and I wonder what the fuss was. Knowing this doesn't stop it, but it shortens it.
