# Goal Setting

What I've learned after years of setting goals I never hit. Mostly about being honest with myself.

## Why Most Of My Goals Failed
- They were wishes, not plans. "Get fit" is a mood, not a goal.
- No next physical action. A goal with no first step is just anxiety.
- I set too many at once and then served none of them.

## What Works Better For Me
- One or two real goals at a time. The rest go on a someday list and wait.
- Tie the goal to a behavior, not an outcome. "Write 4 days a week" beats "publish a book."
- Make it small enough that I can't lie about whether I did it.

## The Outcome vs Process Thing
I can't control outcomes, only what I do daily. So I set process goals and let the outcomes happen or not. This sounds like a cope but it's the only version that ever produced results.

## Checking In
- I review goals weekly, not once a year on January 1st.
- If I keep missing a goal, the goal is wrong, not me. Shrink it or drop it.

## The Skeptical Note
Half of goal-setting advice is motivational theater. The boring truth is: pick few, define the daily action, and show up. Everything else is decoration on top of that.
