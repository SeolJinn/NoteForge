# Inbox Zero

The procedure that makes email manageable. Part of the [[Weekly Review Template]] sweep.

## The Three-Folder Setup

Instead of fifty Gmail labels, three:

- **Inbox.** Things that need a decision.
- **Followup.** Sent emails I'm waiting on a reply for.
- **Reference.** Anything I might need to find later.

Everything that hits the inbox gets one of these treatments — reply, archive to reference, schedule, or delete. No fourth option.

## Two-Minute Rule

If a reply takes under two minutes, write it now. Otherwise schedule a real time to handle it. Triage is the work; replying is just the visible part.

## Subscriptions

- Use a separate "newsletter" address routed to a folder. The main inbox is for humans.
- Unsubscribe ruthlessly the second a newsletter stops being read. Future-you will not catch up.

## The "Zero" Part

The goal isn't an empty inbox forever. It's an empty inbox at the end of each session. If you can't process to zero in 20 minutes, the inflow is wrong, not the procedure.

## What Doesn't Belong in Email

Anything that needs back-and-forth. Anything that needs a group decision. Anything emotional. Move those conversations to the right channel — chat, a call, in person.

## The Reset

If you fall a thousand emails behind, declare bankruptcy. Archive the lot. Anything truly important will surface again.
