# Note Taking Systems

Every system I've tried and what actually stuck. Mostly a graveyard with a few survivors.

## Systems I Abandoned
- **Zettelkasten.** Beautiful in theory. I spent more time linking than thinking.
- **Bullet journal.** The drawing-the-grid ritual became the hobby instead of the work.
- **One giant daily note.** Searchable, but nothing was ever findable later.

## What Actually Stuck
- One note per idea, named like a sentence so I can read titles like a list.
- Links only when they're real, not to feel productive.
- Capture fast and messy, clean up later or never.

## The Capture vs Organize Split
The capture should be frictionless. The organizing should be deliberate and rare. I kept mixing them and that's why systems collapsed. Now I capture all day and tidy once a week.

## Rules I Keep Breaking
- Don't start a note you won't finish thinking through. (I break this constantly.)
- A note nobody will reread is just a diary entry. That's fine, just be honest about it.

## What I Actually Believe
The system matters way less than the habit of writing things down at all. I've made worse systems work and better systems fail. The tool is not the point.
