# Tokyo Itinerary

Seven days, second-time visitor. See [[Packing List]] and [[Travel Photography Tips]].

## Neighborhoods Worth a Half Day Each

- **Shimokitazawa.** Vintage shops, tiny live music venues, no tourists.
- **Yanaka.** The old Tokyo that survived the bombings. Cemetery walk, tiny galleries, the world's most committed cat population.
- **Kichijōji.** Inokashira Park, the Ghibli Museum (book months ahead), Harmonica Yokocho at night.
- **Shimo-Kitazawa is not the same as Shimokita.** It is. Just spelled differently. Don't let map searches confuse you.

## Food Plan

- One sushi splurge: Sushi Sho in Yotsuya, omakase, book six weeks out.
- One ramen meal per day, varied: tonkotsu, shoyu, tsukemen, abura soba.
- Konbini breakfast — egg sandwich at 7-Eleven, onigiri at Lawson, oden in winter.
- Department store food halls (depachika) at closing time for half-price bento.

## Trains

- Suica or Pasmo on your phone. Apple Wallet works as of 2024. Top up via the app.
- The JR Pass is no longer cheap; calculate carefully. Often a regional pass is better.
- Last train is around midnight. Miss it and you're walking or taxi-ing.

## What I'd Skip Next Time

- Shibuya Sky. The view is real but the queue and the price aren't.
- Most of Akihabara unless you have a specific shop in mind.
- The TeamLab museums on a busy day. They're an Instagram queue.
