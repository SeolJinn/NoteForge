# Lisbon Trip

Five days, late October. See [[Packing List]] before you go.

## Where to Stay

Alfama if you want atmosphere, Príncipe Real if you want quiet, Cais do Sodré if you want to be near the train. Avoid Bairro Alto unless you genuinely want to be woken at 3am.

## Itinerary That Worked

- **Day 1.** Fly in, settle, pastel de nata at Manteigaria. Walk down to Praça do Comércio at sunset.
- **Day 2.** São Jorge castle in the morning before the cruise crowd. Lunch at Cervejaria Ramiro. Tram 28 to Estrela.
- **Day 3.** Day trip to Sintra. Quinta da Regaleira beats Pena Palace if you have to pick one.
- **Day 4.** LX Factory in the morning, MAAT in the afternoon. Sunset at Miradouro da Senhora do Monte.
- **Day 5.** Belém — Jerónimos, the original Pastéis de Belém (yes the queue is worth it once).

## Food Notes

- Bifana at O Trevo on Praça Luís de Camões. Two euros, perfect.
- Ginjinha at A Ginjinha by Rossio. One shot, standing, then leave.
- Skip anywhere with a multilingual menu and a host outside.

## Logistics

- Get a Viva Viagem card at any metro station — works on metro, bus, tram, suburban trains.
- Trams are charming and crowded. Time your tourist-tram rides for early or late.
- ATMs marked "Multibanco" are bank-operated and don't gouge on fees. Avoid the standalone "Euronet" ones.
