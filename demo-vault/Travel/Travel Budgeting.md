# Travel Budgeting

How I keep trips affordable without traveling miserably. See [[Carry-On Only]].

## Where the Money Actually Goes
- Flights and accommodation are usually 60 percent of the trip. Get those right and the rest is noise.
- The daily spend creeps up on coffees, snacks, and one-more-drinks. Track it for the first three days and you'll see the pattern.

## Flights
- Set fare alerts six weeks out for short haul, three months for long. Tuesday and Wednesday departures run cheaper.
- Budget airline base fares are a trap until you add a bag and a seat. Price the full total before you celebrate.

## Accommodation
- A place with a kitchen pays for itself by the third breakfast. Eating every meal out is the silent budget killer.
- Location over luxury. A plain room you walk home to beats a nice one that needs a 20 euro taxi each night.

## Daily Spending
- Pull local cash from a bank ATM, decline the machine's currency conversion, always. That "convenience" rate costs you 5 to 10 percent.
- Carry a card with no foreign transaction fee. Over two weeks the fees add up to a nice dinner.

## What I Don't Cheap Out On
- The thing I came for. If it's a city for food, I eat well. If it's mountains, I pay for the good guide.
- Travel insurance on anything far from home. One missed connection or clinic visit erases the savings.
