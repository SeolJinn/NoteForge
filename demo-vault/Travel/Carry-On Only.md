# Carry-On Only

How I travel for two weeks with one 40-liter bag. See [[Packing List]] and [[Long Haul Flight Tips]].

## The Case for It
- No checked bag means no waiting at the carousel, no lost luggage, no fees on budget airlines.
- You can run for a connection. You can change a plan on the spot. The freedom is the whole point.

## The Bag
- 40 liters or under. A panel-loading backpack beats a top-loader for finding things without unpacking everything.
- Soft-sided so it squashes into the sizer. Ryanair and Wizz will measure it, and they're not bluffing.

## Clothes That Earn Their Place
- Merino wool. Two t-shirts you can wear four days each and rinse in a sink. Worth the price.
- One layering system: tee, long sleeve, light puffer, rain shell. Covers most climates.
- Three pairs of socks and underwear, washed every couple of nights. Quick-dry, never cotton.

## Toiletries
- Bars over bottles. Solid shampoo and soap aren't liquids, so they skip the 100ml limit entirely.
- A toothbrush, deodorant stick, and sunscreen cover it. Everything else you can buy there.

## What Stays Home
- The "just in case" items. You'll buy them if you ever need them, which you won't.
- The second pair of shoes. Wear the bulky ones, pack one light pair, done.
