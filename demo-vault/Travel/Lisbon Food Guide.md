# Lisbon Food Guide

Where I actually eat when I'm in town, built up over four visits. See [[Lisbon Trip]].

## Pastéis de Nata
- Manteigaria over Pastéis de Belém. Same era of recipe, half the queue, and they bake them all day so you get one warm.
- Eat it standing at the counter with cinnamon and a bica. Sitting down adds a euro for nothing.

## Tascas Worth the Detour
- **Zé dos Cornos**, Mouraria. Grilled meats, paper tablecloths, locals on their lunch break. Under 15 euros full.
- **O Trevo**, Praça Luís de Camões. The bifana Bourdain ate. Order it with mustard and a beer, stand at the bar.

## Tinned Fish
- It isn't a tourist gimmick, it's dinner. Buy from Conserveira de Lisboa, not the souvenir shops on Rua Augusta.
- Sardines in olive oil, a tin of mackerel, good bread, a tomato. That's a meal on a hot day.

## Markets
- Time Out Market is fine but it's a food court with marketing. Go once, early, before the cruise crowds.
- Mercado de Campo de Ourique is where I actually go. Smaller, calmer, real produce stalls mixed with the stands.

## A Few Rules
- Lunch is the value meal. The prato do dia at any tasca beats dinner prices everywhere.
- Coffee is a bica, not an espresso, and it costs under a euro. Never pay 3 euros for one on a square.
- Vinho verde, chilled, by the glass. Cheap and it suits the climate.
