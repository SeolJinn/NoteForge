# Train Travel in Europe

Why I take the train across Europe and how to do it without overpaying. See [[Travel Budgeting]].

## Why Train Over Plane
- City center to city center, no airport two hours out, no security theater. Often faster door to door under 700km.
- You see the country instead of clouds. The Alps from a Swiss train window justify the ticket alone.

## Booking
- High-speed tickets get cheaper the earlier you book, like flights. The cheap fares for TGV and Frecciarossa open three months out.
- Regional and most German trains are flat-priced. No rush, buy them the day before or at the station.
- Use the national operator sites or Trainline. Avoid the resellers tacking on fees.

## Passes vs Point-to-Point
- A Eurail or Interrail pass only wins if you're moving most days across many countries.
- For a focused two-city trip, individual advance tickets are almost always cheaper. Do the math before you buy the pass.
- Reservations are mandatory on high-speed and night trains even with a pass, and they cost extra.

## Night Trains
- The Nightjet network is back and good. A couchette saves a hotel night and wakes you up somewhere new.
- Book the early sleeper berths fast. They sell out and the seats-only option is a long, rough night.

## On the Day
- Validate regional paper tickets if the machine asks. In Italy a non-validated ticket is a fine, full stop.
- Coaches are numbered along the platform. Find your coach marker before the train pulls in or you'll sprint.
