# Travel Photography Tips

Notes from carrying too much gear and slowly carrying less. Linked from [[Tokyo Itinerary]].

## Gear

One body, one prime, maybe a second prime. A 35mm equivalent is the most versatile single lens for travel — wide enough for streets, tight enough for portraits. A second lens, if any, should be a 50mm or 85mm for portraits and details. Zooms are honest and uninspiring.

## The Hour That Actually Matters

Golden hour is overrated for cities and underrated for landscapes. For street photography, the hour right after dawn beats every sunset — light is the same quality but the streets are empty.

## Composition Discipline

- Get closer. Then closer.
- The subject is rarely the building. It's the person reacting to the building.
- Backgrounds matter as much as subjects. Walk three steps left.
- If you have to crop heavily, you stood in the wrong place.

## Settings I Default To

- Aperture priority, f/4 or f/5.6, auto-ISO with a 1/200 minimum shutter, exposure compensation -1/3.
- RAW always. JPEG previews are fine for sharing on the phone same-day.

## Editing

- Lightroom on the desktop, never on the phone for keepers.
- White balance first, exposure second, contrast third. Most other sliders are vanity.
- Black and white is not a fix for a bad color shot.

## What I Stopped Doing

Carrying a tripod for everything. A tripod earns its place once a trip — for blue hour cityscapes — and otherwise sits in the bag.
