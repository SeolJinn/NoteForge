# Rome in Three Days

A first-timer's three days that doesn't try to see everything. Pairs with [[Travel Budgeting]].

## Day One: Ancient Rome
- Colosseum and Forum on one combined ticket. Book the timed slot online days ahead or you'll lose an hour in line.
- Walk the Forum in the morning, then up Palatine Hill for the view. Bring water, there's no shade.
- Evening: Trastevere for dinner. Cobbled, loud, full of life. Cacio e pepe at a place with no English menu.

## Day Two: The Vatican
- Sistine Chapel first thing or last thing. The midday crush is real and joyless.
- St. Peter's is free but the dome climb costs a few euros and is worth every step. 551 stairs if you skip the lift.
- Dress code is enforced. Shoulders and knees covered or they turn you away at the door.

## Day Three: The Wandering Day
- Pantheon, Trevi, Spanish Steps on foot. They're closer together than the map suggests.
- Throw the coin over your left shoulder at Trevi if you must, early, before the crowds three-deep.
- Coffee standing at the bar costs a third of the price of sitting. Order an espresso, not a cappuccino after 11.

## Eating
- Skip any restaurant with a host waving a menu at you near a monument. Walk two streets back.
- Gelato test: if the pistachio is bright green, leave. Real pistachio is dull and grey-green.
