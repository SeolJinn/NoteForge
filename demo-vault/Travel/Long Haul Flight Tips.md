# Long Haul Flight Tips

Things I've learned over too many 11-hour flights. See [[Carry-On Only]] and [[Packing List]].

## Seat Choice
- Window if you want to sleep, aisle if you want to move. Never the middle, ever.
- Check SeatGuru before you pick. The exit row with no recline or the seat by the toilets ruins the trip.
- Row near the front of economy boards last and deplanes early. Worth it.

## Sleep
- Set your watch to the destination time at the gate, not on landing. Decide then whether this is a sleep flight.
- Eye mask, real earplugs, and a neck pillow that actually wraps. The foam horseshoe does nothing.
- Skip the second meal if it lands near your destination's morning. Sleep is worth more than the tray.

## The Body
- Water every couple of hours, get up and walk every two. The free wine dehydrates you and wrecks the next day.
- Compression socks aren't vanity on a 12-hour leg. Cheap insurance against swollen ankles.

## Carry-On Survival Kit
- Toothbrush, a clean shirt, phone charger, and any medication in the bag under the seat, not the overhead.
- Download everything before you board. Plane wifi is slow or absent and always overpriced.

## Landing Day
- Get sunlight as soon as you arrive. It resets your clock faster than anything.
- Don't nap longer than 30 minutes that first afternoon or you'll be awake at 3am.
