# Hiking the Dolomites

Notes from a week of hut-to-hut walking in the Italian Alps. See [[Packing List]].

## When to Go
- Late June to mid-September. The huts open around the third week of June and close by early October.
- July and August are busy and warm. September is my pick: clearer air, thinner crowds, colder nights.

## The Hut System
- Rifugios are the whole reason to go. You walk between them with a light pack and sleep at altitude.
- Book ahead in summer, especially Lagazuoi and the popular Tre Cime loops. They fill weeks out.
- Bring a sleeping bag liner and cash. Many huts are card-shy and a liner is required in the dorms.

## The Walking
- Tre Cime di Lavaredo is the classic loop. Three hours, mostly level, the most famous skyline in the range.
- The Alta Via 1 is the long one. Ten days north to south if you want a real trek, hut to hut the whole way.
- Via ferrata is everywhere. If you want the cabled routes, rent a harness and kit in Cortina and learn the basics first.

## Kit
- Layers and a real rain shell. Afternoon storms roll in fast above 2,000 meters even on a clear morning.
- Poles save your knees on the long descents. Boots, not trail runners, for the scree.

## Logistics
- Cortina d'Ampezzo is the obvious base. No train, so it's a bus from Calalzo or a drive.
- Buses connect the trailheads but run on alpine summer schedules. Check the last one back before you commit to a long day.
