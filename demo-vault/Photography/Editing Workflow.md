# Editing Workflow

How I get from a card full of RAWs to ten keepers without losing a weekend.

## Cull First, Hard

- Import everything, then make one fast pass flagging only the obvious yeses.
- Second pass on the flagged set, picking the single best of each near-duplicate.
- Most shoots give me three or four real keepers from hundreds. That's normal. Delete the rest without ceremony.

Culling is the skill. The editing is the easy part once the bad ones are gone.

## The Order I Edit In

1. White balance — get the color honest before anything else.
2. Exposure and the highlight/shadow recovery.
3. Contrast and the tone curve for mood.
4. Local adjustments — a mask to lift a face, dim a hot sky.
5. Crop and straighten last, once I know what the photo is.

Color comes near the end, and sparingly. Most other sliders are vanity.

## My Tools

- Lightroom on the desktop for the catalog and the bulk of the work.
- Photoshop only for cloning out something distracting that a mask can't fix.
- Never serious editing on the phone. Phone edits are for same-day sharing, not keepers.

## Presets

I keep two or three of my own as starting points, never as the finish. A preset gets the file 60% there; the last 40% is per-photo and can't be automated.

## What I Stopped Doing

- Over-editing. The HDR-clarity-saturation look ages badly. I dial back until it looks like I barely touched it.
- Keeping rejects "just in case." They pile up and I never revisit them.
- Treating black and white as a rescue for a weak color shot. It isn't.
