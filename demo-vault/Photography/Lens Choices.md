# Lens Choices

What I own, what I reach for, and what I wasted money on.

## The One-Lens Answer

If I could keep one, it's a 35mm prime. Wide enough for a room or a street, tight enough for an environmental portrait, small enough to carry all day. It forces me to move my feet, which makes me a better photographer than any zoom ever did.

## Primes I Actually Use

- **35mm f/1.8** — the default. Lives on the camera.
- **50mm f/1.8** — the cheap "nifty fifty." Lovely for portraits, absurd value.
- **85mm f/1.8** — when I want compression and a melted background. Heavier, lives in the bag.

Fast primes earn their keep in low light and in the look they give. The blur from f/1.8 is something a kit zoom simply can't fake.

## When A Zoom Makes Sense

- Events and travel days where I can't change glass constantly.
- A 24-70mm covers the range honestly. Convenient and a little soulless.
- Wildlife or sports — there's no prime substitute for a long zoom you can afford.

## Focal Length, In Feel

- 24mm: dramatic, close, distorts faces. Great for places, risky for people.
- 35mm: how I see. Natural, unfussy.
- 50mm: slightly tighter than the eye, flattering.
- 85mm+: flattering compression, separation, distance from the subject.

## What I Wasted Money On

- A superzoom that did everything adequately and nothing well.
- A specialty lens I used twice. Rent the weird ones before you buy.
- Filters I never use, except a polarizer, which does earn its slot.
