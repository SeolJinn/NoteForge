# Running Form

What I think about when my easy runs start feeling heavy. Related to [[Mobility and Stretching]].

## Cadence
- I aim for around 175 steps per minute. Below 165 my stride gets long and I start heel-slamming.
- Counting right-foot strikes for 30 seconds and doubling it gives a quick check.

## Where I Land
- Foot under the hip, not out in front. The reaching-forward stride is what trashes my knees.
- A slight forward lean from the ankles, not the waist. Falling, not bending.

## Arms and Shoulders
- Hands relaxed, like holding a chip I don't want to crush.
- When my shoulders creep up toward my ears it usually means I'm tired and should slow down.

## Uphill vs Downhill
- Uphill: shorten the stride, lean in, let cadence stay high. Don't power through.
- Downhill is where form falls apart. Quick light steps, no braking with a stiff leg.

## The Tired Test
When the last mile gets ugly I run a quick mental checklist: cadence up, hands loose, eyes on the horizon. Usually two of the three have slipped.
