# Sleep Hygiene

Things that worked. Things that didn't. The list I revisit when sleep gets bad.

## What Helps

- **Consistent wake time.** Even on weekends. Bedtime can drift; wake time should not.
- **Morning light within 30 minutes of waking.** A walk if possible. A bright window if not.
- **No caffeine after noon.** This single rule made the biggest difference. Caffeine half-life is 5-6 hours, longer than people think.
- **Cool bedroom.** 17-19C. Cooler than I would have guessed.
- **Wind-down ritual.** Same sequence each night for the last 45 minutes. Brain learns to associate it with sleep.

## What Doesn't Help

- **Sleep tracking.** The data was interesting and the anxiety it produced was worse than the deficit it measured.
- **Melatonin as a sedative.** It's a circadian signal, not a sleep aid. Useful for jet lag, useless for "I can't fall asleep tonight."
- **Alcohol.** Falls-asleep-faster, sleeps-worse-overall. Net negative for sleep quality even at one drink.

## When Sleep Is Bad

The first thing I check is caffeine timing. The second is screen exposure in the last hour. The third is whatever I was thinking about during the day that I haven't written down.

## The Rule of Getting Up

If I'm in bed for 20 minutes and not asleep, get up. Read in dim light until drowsy. Lying in bed staring at the ceiling teaches the brain that bed is for being awake.

## What I Stopped Worrying About

- Eight hours specifically. Some nights are seven, some are nine. Average matters; any single night doesn't.
- Naps. A 20-minute nap before 3pm has never hurt my night sleep, despite the folk wisdom.
