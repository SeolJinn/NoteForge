# Desk Posture

Notes from fixing the neck ache that came with working from home.

## The Setup
- Top of the monitor at eye level. Raising mine on a stack of books killed the forward head tilt.
- Elbows around 90 degrees, wrists flat. My old too-high desk had me shrugging all day.

## Feet and Hips
- Feet flat on the floor, hips slightly higher than knees. Crossing my legs always creeps back in.
- A small cushion behind my lower back keeps me from sliding into a slump by afternoon.

## The Real Fix
- **Movement beats posture.** No position is good for three hours straight.
- I set a timer to stand every 30 minutes. Even just standing resets the slouch.

## Micro-Breaks
- Chin tucks, shoulder rolls, a doorway chest stretch. Thirty seconds, a few times a day.

## What Actually Worked
The best posture is the next one. Sitting perfectly still just made a different part ache.
