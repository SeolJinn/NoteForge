# Plants I'm Growing

A small apartment, mostly indirect light, slightly chaotic.

## Thriving

- **Pothos.** The unkillable. Three trailing variants on a north-facing shelf. Water when the leaves wilt slightly; otherwise leave alone.
- **Snake plant.** Bedroom corner. Actually thrives on neglect. I water it monthly at most.
- **ZZ plant.** Bathroom. Only mostly visible. Doesn't seem to mind.

## Holding Steady

- **Monstera.** Big and dramatic. Rotates a quarter turn every week so the leaves don't all reach toward the window.
- **Olive tree.** A gift. Ostensibly outdoor, currently indoor, sulks but survives.
- **Three orchids.** One in bloom, two in their off-season "thinking about it" phase. Watered with three ice cubes weekly.

## Struggling

- **Fiddle leaf fig.** Of course. The diva of houseplants. Two new leaves this year, three lost. Net negative.
- **Calathea.** Crisp brown leaf edges no matter what. Tap water is too hard. Need to switch to filtered.

## Recently Lost

- **Succulent collection.** Not enough light. Overwatered after I gave up. Lessons noted.
- **Basil from a supermarket pot.** Lasted six weeks. They never last more than six weeks.

## What I'd Do Differently

- Buy local-grown plants instead of supermarket ones. The supermarket plants are forced for shelf appeal and collapse afterward.
- Group plants by water schedule, not by aesthetic. Aesthetic groupings die in rotation.
- Accept that some species hate my apartment. Stop replacing them with the same species expecting different results.
