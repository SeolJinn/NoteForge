# Managing Stress

What I reach for when the week gets heavy. Some of this overlaps with [[Meditation Notes]].

## Move First
- A 20 minute walk does more for my mood than an hour of stewing at my desk.
- On bad days I lower the bar. Even getting outside for five minutes counts.

## Name It
- Writing down what's actually bothering me usually shrinks it. Vague dread is worse than a named problem.
- Half my stress turns out to be three small things I kept circling instead of listing.

## Cut the Inputs
- Less news, fewer open tabs, phone in another room. The noise stacks up faster than I notice.
- I stopped checking email after dinner. The problems were still there in the morning, calmer.

## Sleep Is the Multiplier
- Everything is harder on bad sleep. When stressed I protect bedtime first.

## What Doesn't Help
Doom-scrolling and pretending I'm fine. Both feel productive in the moment and cost me later.
