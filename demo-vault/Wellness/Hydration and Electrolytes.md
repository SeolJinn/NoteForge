# Hydration and Electrolytes

Notes from figuring out why long runs sometimes wrecked me. See also [[Running Form]].

## My Baseline
- Roughly 2 to 2.5 liters of water on a normal day. More when it's hot or I've trained.
- Pale yellow is the only gauge I actually trust. Clear means I overdid it.

## When Water Isn't Enough
- On runs over 60 minutes, plain water alone left me foggy and crampy.
- Adding sodium changed everything. A pinch of salt and some juice in my bottle did the job.

## The Salty Sweater Problem
- My shirts dry stiff and white. That's a sign I lose a lot of sodium and need to replace it.
- Electrolyte tabs on long hot days. I skip them for anything under an hour.

## Morning Habit
- A glass of water before coffee. I wake up mildly dehydrated and coffee doesn't fix that.

## What I Got Wrong
Chugging a liter right before a run. It just sloshed. Small sips earlier in the day work better.
