# Workout Plan

Three days a week, full-body, conservative volume. The plan I keep coming back to after experimenting and overdoing it.

## The Sessions

### Day A — Push

- Bench press: 3×5
- Overhead press: 3×8
- Dumbbell incline press: 3×10
- Triceps pushdown: 3×12
- Lateral raises: 3×12

### Day B — Pull

- Deadlift: 3×5
- Pull-ups: 3×AMRAP
- Barbell row: 3×8
- Face pulls: 3×15
- Curls: 3×10

### Day C — Legs

- Back squat: 3×5
- Romanian deadlift: 3×8
- Walking lunges: 3×10 per leg
- Calf raises: 3×15
- Plank: 3×60s

## Progression

Add 2.5kg to the main lift once I can hit all sets at the top of the rep range. Don't add weight on accessory lifts — drop the weight when form breaks down.

## What's Worked

- **Three days, not five.** Five days collapsed when life got busy. Three is the floor I always hit.
- **Compound lifts first.** Energy is highest at the start of a session. Spend it on the things that matter.
- **Deload weeks.** Every six weeks, halve the volume. Comes back stronger.

## What Hasn't

- Cardio "on top of" lifting. Either I commit to one or both suffer.
- Tracking RPE. The number was a distraction; I do better trusting feel.
- Pre-workout supplements. Glorified caffeine and a placebo. I've gone back to coffee.
