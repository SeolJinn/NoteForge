# Index Fund Investing

My personal notes on why I keep things boring and broad with index funds.

## Why I went this route

I tried picking individual stocks for a while and mostly underperformed.

- Less time spent watching tickers, more time living my life
- Broad funds spread the risk across hundreds of companies
- The fees on the funds I hold are tiny, which compounds in my favor
- I don't have to be right about any single company

## How I keep it simple

My whole approach fits on an index card, which is kind of the point.

- A broad market fund as the core holding
- A smaller slice of international exposure
- I rebalance maybe once or twice a year, not more
- Automatic contributions on payday so I never see the money

## The habit that matters most

Honestly the strategy matters less than just keeping at it.

- I invest the same amount every month regardless of headlines
- I don't time the market, I'm consistently bad at predicting it
- Down months are buying opportunities, I try to remember that

## Mistakes I made early

- Checking the balance daily and panicking on red days
- Chasing whatever fund did well last year
- Selling during a dip and missing the recovery

These are personal notes from my own experience, not advice for anyone else.
