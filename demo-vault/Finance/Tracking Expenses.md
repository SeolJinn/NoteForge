# Tracking Expenses

How I keep an eye on where the money goes, which feeds straight into [[Budgeting Basics]].

## My low-effort method

I tried tracking every single purchase and burned out in two weeks.

- Now I just review transactions once a week, not log them live
- Sunday evening, ten minutes, scroll through the card statement
- Tag anything weird or wrong, ignore the obvious stuff

The goal is patterns, not a perfect ledger of every snack.

## What I look for

The weekly scan is really about catching surprises.

- Subscriptions I forgot about and don't use anymore
- A category that's quietly ballooning (takeout, every time)
- Duplicate or wrong charges I'd never have noticed otherwise

## The categories I bother with

I keep these deliberately coarse so reviewing is fast.

- Groceries, eating out, transport, bills, fun, other
- Anything that doesn't fit goes in "other" and I don't sweat it
- If "other" gets big, that's a sign I need a new bucket

## What changed once I started

- I cancelled three subscriptions in the first month alone
- Eating out turned out to be way more than I'd have guessed
- Seeing the numbers made cutting back feel less like deprivation

Tracking only works because it's cheap enough that I actually keep doing it.
