# Tax Notes

My running notes so next tax season doesn't catch me scrambling like last year.

## The folder I keep all year

The single best thing I do is collect documents as they arrive.

- One folder, digital and physical, for anything tax-related
- Income statements, deduction receipts, charity confirmations
- I drop things in the moment I get them, not in a panic in spring

Past me always hates present me for losing a receipt I needed.

## Deductions I tend to forget

Every year I rediscover the same ones too late.

- Work-from-home costs and a slice of certain bills
- Charitable donations I made and didn't log
- Professional memberships and courses related to my work

Now I keep a running list here the moment I spend something deductible.

## My timeline

- Documents trickle in through winter, I file them as they come
- Early in the season I do a first pass to see what's missing
- I leave a buffer before the deadline, never the night before

## Things I learned the hard way

- Guessing numbers from memory leads to errors and stress
- Keeping the prior year's return handy makes this year faster
- A boring labeled folder beats a clever system I won't maintain

These are just my own organizing notes, not tax advice for anyone else.
