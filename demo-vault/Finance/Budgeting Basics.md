# Budgeting Basics

How I actually keep my spending under control without obsessing over every coffee.

## The system I landed on

After trying a dozen apps I settled on a simple monthly plan I update by hand.

- Income at the top, fixed costs next, then everything flexible
- I budget in rough buckets, not line items
- Rent, utilities, groceries, transport, fun money, savings
- If a bucket runs dry I borrow from fun money, never from savings

The point is awareness, not precision. I don't care if I'm off by a few units.

## Why I budget at all

I used to hit the end of the month and have no idea where the money went.

- Now I can see which categories quietly creep up (groceries, always)
- It makes saying no to impulse buys easier when I see the bucket
- I sleep better knowing the fixed costs are covered before anything else

## My monthly ritual

First weekend of the month I sit down with coffee and reset the plan.

- Copy last month's template, adjust for anything unusual coming up
- Travel month? Pull from fun money in advance so I'm not surprised
- Check whether any subscriptions snuck back onto the card

## What I stopped doing

- I stopped budgeting in tiny categories, it was too much upkeep
- I stopped feeling guilty about fun money, that's the whole point of it
- I stopped trusting auto-categorization and started reviewing it myself

The boring version that I actually maintain beats the perfect one I abandon.
