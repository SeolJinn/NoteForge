# Emergency Fund

The boring cash pile that has saved me more stress than any investment ever has.

## What it's for

This is the money I touch only when something actually goes wrong.

- Car repair, broken laptop, a surprise medical bill
- A gap between jobs so I'm not making desperate decisions
- Not for vacations, not for a sale, not for "I deserve it"

The whole value is that it sits there being boring until I genuinely need it.

## How big I keep it

I aim for a few months of essential expenses, not my full lifestyle.

- I worked out my bare-bones monthly number first (rent, food, utilities)
- Multiplied that, didn't include fun money in the calculation
- Started small and built it up over many months without rushing

## Where I park it

I want it accessible but slightly annoying to reach.

- A separate savings account, not my main checking
- Earns a bit of interest but I picked it for safety, not returns
- No card attached so I can't tap it on a whim

## Rebuilding after using it

- When I dip into it, refilling becomes the top savings priority again
- I pause other goals until it's back to a level I'm comfortable with
- Every time I've used it I've been grateful past me set it up

Once it's funded I mostly forget about it, which is exactly the goal.
