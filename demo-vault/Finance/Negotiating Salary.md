# Negotiating Salary

Notes to myself from the times I did this well and the times I fumbled it.

## Do the homework first

The negotiations that went well were the ones I prepared for.

- Look up real ranges for the role and region before talking numbers
- Write down my actual accomplishments with concrete results
- Know my walk-away number and my happy number in advance

Walking in without research means accepting whatever they offer first.

## Let them name a number when I can

I've learned to deflect the early "what are you looking for?" politely.

- I ask what range they have budgeted for the role
- If pushed, I give a range with my target near the bottom
- I never blurt out my current pay as the anchor

## When the offer comes

The first offer is rarely the best one they can do.

- I always pause and thank them before responding
- I counter once, calmly, with a specific number and reasons
- I ask about the whole package, not just base pay

## Things beyond the base number

- Extra time off can matter more to me than a small raise
- Flexible schedule, learning budget, equipment all count
- I get the final agreement in writing before I'm happy

## What I tell myself

- Asking is not rude, it's expected, the worst answer is no
- Silence after I state a number is fine, I let it sit
- My value doesn't drop just because asking feels awkward
