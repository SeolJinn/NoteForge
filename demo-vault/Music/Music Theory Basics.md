# Music Theory Basics

Trying to understand *why* chords sound good together instead of just memorizing shapes. Slow going but it's clicking.

## The Stuff That Finally Made Sense

- A major scale is just W-W-H-W-W-W-H. That pattern is everything.
- Chords are built by stacking every other note of a scale (1-3-5).
- The key of C has no sharps or flats, which is why everyone starts there.
- Minor chords just flatten the third. That one note changes the whole mood.

## The Number System

- I-IV-V is the backbone of basically every folk and blues song.
- In the key of G that's G, C, D. Suddenly I see it everywhere.
- The vi chord (Em in C) is the "sad but pretty" option.
- Roman numerals let me transpose without re-learning a song.

## The Circle of Fifths

- Still wrapping my head around this but the clockwise = add a sharp rule helps.
- Adjacent keys share most of their notes, which is why they sound related.
- I drew one out and taped it above my desk.

## Where I Get Stuck

- Modes. I can recite them but I can't hear them yet.
- Knowing when a song borrows a chord from another key.
- Reading standard notation. I lean on tabs way too much.

## Goal

- Be able to figure out a song's chords by ear within a few tries.
- Understand a ii-V-I before I attempt any jazz.
