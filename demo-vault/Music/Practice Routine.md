# Practice Routine

My 30-minute daily block that actually keeps me improving on [[Learning Guitar]]. Theory work folds in from [[Music Theory Basics]].

## The Block (30 min)

- 5 min: warmup. Chromatic finger exercise up and down the neck.
- 10 min: chord changes with the metronome. One problem pair per day.
- 10 min: working on a song, hands separate if fingerpicking.
- 5 min: just play something fun so I end on a high note.

## Rules I Set Myself

- Same time every day, right after morning coffee. Habit beats motivation.
- If I miss a day, I don't try to "make up" 60 minutes. Just resume.
- Metronome stays on for the middle 20 minutes whether I like it or not.
- No phone on the music stand. It's a black hole.

## Tracking

- I log each session in a notebook: BPM hit, what felt hard.
- Tied this into my [[Habits I'm Tracking]] list so I see the streak.
- Currently on a 23-day run. Longest yet.

## What I Rotate Weekly

- Monday/Thursday: barre chord drills (the F, the Bm).
- Tuesday/Friday: a new song section.
- Wednesday: pure theory, away from the guitar.
- Weekend: looser, just play and explore.

## Honest Notes

- The 5-minute fun ending is doing more for consistency than anything else.
- I improve fastest on the days I slow the metronome *down*, not up.
