# Learning Guitar

Picking this back up after a decade away, and this time I'm sticking with it. See [[Practice Routine]] for the daily grind.

## Where I'm At

- Started in February on a borrowed Yamaha FG800, bought my own in April.
- Comfortable with open chords now: C, G, D, Em, Am, E, A.
- F barre is still my nemesis. About one clean attempt in four.
- Switching between G and C still loses me half a beat.

## What's Actually Working

- Slowing everything down with a metronome at 60 BPM, no exceptions.
- Practicing chord changes as pairs, not whole songs. G to D, fifty reps.
- Anchoring my pinky on the high E so my hand stops drifting.
- Filing my nails on the fretting hand every Sunday. Huge difference.

## Songs I Can Play

- "Horse with No Name" (just two chords, great for confidence)
- "Knockin' on Heaven's Door"
- "Wonderwall" if I capo the 2nd fret and ignore the strumming
- Working toward "Blackbird" but the fingerpicking is months away

## Gear Notes

- D'Addario EJ16 phosphor bronze, light gauge. Easier on my fingertips.
- Snark clip-on tuner. The free apps were close but not close enough.
- A capo changed everything. Suddenly half the songs are in my range.

## Next Up

- Get the F barre clean before summer.
- Learn the basic pentatonic shape so I can noodle.
- Stop avoiding songs just because they have a B minor.
