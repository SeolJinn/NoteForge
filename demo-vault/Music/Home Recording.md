# Home Recording

Setting up a corner of the spare room to record my guitar without spending a fortune. Mostly works now.

## My Setup

- Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 interface. The classic starter choice for a reason.
- An SM58 I already owned, plus a borrowed condenser for acoustic.
- Reaper as the DAW. Cheap license, runs on my aging laptop fine.
- A pop filter made from a coat hanger and old tights. Looks terrible, works.

## Room Treatment On A Budget

- Two moving blankets on the wall behind me killed most of the slap echo.
- Recording in the closet for vocals. Cramped but dead quiet.
- Moved the desk off the wall a few inches. Surprising bass difference.

## Miking The Acoustic

- Condenser pointed at the 12th fret, not the soundhole. Less boom.
- About a hand's width back. Closer gets muddy fast.
- A second mic at the bridge gives me options in the mix later.

## Workflow Lessons

- Record at 24-bit, leave tons of headroom. Aim for peaks around -12 dB.
- Play to a click track or every layered take drifts.
- Comp the best bits from three takes instead of chasing one perfect run.
- Reference a commercial track in the same genre while mixing.

## Still Figuring Out

- Compression. I overdo it every single time.
- Getting vocals to sit *in* the mix instead of floating on top.
- Latency when monitoring with plugins. Direct monitoring helps for now.
